History

Chicken 65 is a deep-fried Chicken dish that originates from Hotel Buharai, India in 1965. People argue about where the 65 comes from but it is widely agreed that it comes from the year it was first made. As people came and tried Chicken 65 at the restaurant, the popularity increased and the recipe spread all across India with new variations and methods being discovered. Today, Chicken 65 is known to be a crunch and spicy dish that can be used for a main dish, side dish, or even just a snack.

Ingredients

  • 1-1.3 pounds of boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp of corn flour
  • 1/2 tsp of black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp of cumin powder
  • 1 tsp of coriander
  • 2 tbsp of yogurt
  • 1 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp ketchup
  • 3/4-1 tsp of salt
    • To taste
  • 2 green chillies
  • 1-2 cups of canola oil
    • Depends on frying method

Directions


  1. Chop the boneless chicken thighs into the desired amount of pieces.
  2. In a bowl mix the yogurt, egg, corn flour, soy sauce, ketchup, black pepper, cumin powder, chopped green chillies, and salt. Add the chicken to this bowl.
    • Optionally marinate for an hour
  3. Heat oil in a pan, preferably a wok. Fry the chicken 6-8 minutes until it is golden and crispy. Remove and place on a paper towel to dry.
  4. Add small amounts of yogurt, soy sauce, and ketchup to a bowl and toss the chicken for 1 minute to coat lightly. Remove it and add it to a bowl.
  5. Finish with a sprinkle of coriander.
